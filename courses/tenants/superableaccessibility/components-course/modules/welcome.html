Welcome: Hands‑On, Sight Off: Working with NVDA

This micro‑course gives you a practical way to connect visual design with the real experience of using a screen reader.

The Learner’s Challenge

This course is built for NVDA. To get the most value from each lesson, use the same methods your blind users rely on:

  • Turn on NVDA. Use it throughout the course. (Don't have NVDA? View the video below for how to install it. Windows only.)
  • Remove your sight (if applicable). Close your eyes or turn off your monitor when you explore the “Inaccessible” examples.
  • Listen for what NVDA tells you. Notice the roles, states, and properties that make the accessible versions work.

Working without sight helps you notice the “black box” problems that many learners face — missing feedback, lost focus, and reading orders that break your flow. These moments show why inclusive design matters.

How to Use This Course

Each lesson starts with an Inaccessible Example. Explore it first and note what creates barriers. Then select Reveal Accessible Version to see the corrected pattern. Compare the two versions to understand the changes that make the interaction work.
