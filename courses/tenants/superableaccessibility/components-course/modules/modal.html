Modal Dialog

Modals are interactive components that sit on top of the main content. They are excellent for focus, but dangerous for accessibility if they don't trap focus and block background interaction.

Practice Task

1. Activate the accessible modal and identify the functional cues that indicate its role and initial focus.

2. Attempt to Tab outside of the modal and verify that your focus remains trapped within the dialog boundaries.

3. Use the Escape key and identify the functional cues that confirm focus has returned to the trigger button.

Broken Modal

Try tabbing now. You'll find you can still click things on the main page, and your screen reader has no idea this content appeared!

Why it's broken

  • No Initial Focus: When the modal opens, focus remains on the trigger. A screen reader user has no idea a new dialog has appeared unless they manually hunt for it.
  • No Focus Trap: A modal must keep the user's focus within its boundaries until it is closed.
  • Background Operability: The underlying page remains interactive, which can lead to users accidentally navigating away while the modal is "open."
  • No Escape Key: Standard accessibility patterns require the Escape key to close a modal instantly.

Compliance Failures

  • SC 2.4.3: Focus Order - Focus is not managed or trapped. It fails to move to the modal upon opening and does not prevent navigation to background content.
  • SC 2.1.1: Keyboard - The modal lacks keyboard support for closing (Escape key) and does not prevent interaction with background elements.
  • SC 4.1.2: Name, Role, Value - The div lacks the dialog role and an accessible name.

Accessible Example

The accessible version uses the native <dialog> element. This handles focus trapping, backdrop dimming, and the Escape key automatically.

You've learned about tables, carousels, and now modals. These are the building blocks of interactive learning.

By using the <dialog> element, you've ensured that this information is reachable and controllable by everyone.

How it's fixed

  • Native Dialog: Calling dialog.showModal() puts the browser into a specialized "modal" mode.
  • Automatic Inert: Everything outside the <dialog> is automatically made inert (unreachable) by the browser.
  • Focus Management: Focus is automatically moved into the modal when it opens, and returned to the trigger button when it closes.

Compliance Success

  • SC 2.4.3: Focus Order - Focus is automatically managed, trapped within the modal, and returned to the trigger on close.
  • SC 2.1.1: Keyboard - Native <dialog> provides built-in keyboard support and focus trapping.
