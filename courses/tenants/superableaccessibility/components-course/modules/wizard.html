Multi-Step Form / Wizard

Complex tasks are often broken into smaller, sequential steps to reduce cognitive load. This is known as a Wizard pattern. However, without proper structure, screen reader users can lose track of their progress or fail to realize that they have moved to a new step.

Practice Task

1. Navigate through the accessible wizard and identify the functional cues that announce each new step and current progress.

2. Identify how focus management ensures the screen reader immediately announces the title of the new step upon transition.

3. Tab into the input fields and identify the functional cues provided by the fieldset and legend structure.

Inaccessible Example

In this broken version, the fields are just loosely placed in div containers. Stepping "Next" changes the content, but focus stays on the "Next" button, leaving the user with no idea what the new fields are. Additionally, some inputs lack programmatic labels entirely.

Why it's broken

  • No Logical Grouping: There are no <fieldset> or <legend> tags to group the fields semantically.
  • Unlabeled Controls: The "Email Address" field uses a <span> instead of a <label>. NVDA will simply announce "Edit, blank" when tabbing into it.
  • Lack of Progress Orientation: Sighted users see "Step 1" vs "Step 2," but NVDA users aren't automatically notified of the step change because focus never moved.
  • Dangling Focus: Clicking "Next" updates the screen, but the user is still focused on a button that might have changed its label or position, creating confusion.

Compliance Failures

  • SC 1.3.1: Info and Relationships - The grouping of related form fields and the current progress are not programmatically defined. Also, the label and input relationship is missing.
  • SC 3.3.2: Labels or Instructions - Form controls are not programmatically labeled, making them difficult for screen reader users to identify.
  • SC 2.4.3: Focus Order - Lack of focus management when switching steps prevents a logical navigation path.

Accessible Example

The accessible version uses a Progress Bar (via role="progressbar") and explicit Step Headings that receive focus upon transition.

How it's fixed

  • Role="progressbar": We use a native ARIA role to announce the current completion percentage and step count (e.g., "Step 1 of 3").
  • Focus management: When "Next" is pressed, focus moves to the <h3> of the new step. This forces NVDA to read the new step title and number immediately.
  • Fieldsets and Legends: Each step uses semantic grouping to ensure that the relationship between fields is clear.
  • Proper Labels: Every input has a programmatic <label> linked via for and id.

Compliance Success

  • SC 1.3.1: Info and Relationships - Using fieldsets, legends, and progress roles programmatically defines the form structure and progress.
  • SC 3.3.2: Labels or Instructions - Every form control is properly labeled, providing clear context for all users.
  • SC 2.4.3: Focus Order - Moving focus to the new step heading ensures the user starts at the beginning of the new content.
